**Traders can maximize win rates by identifying "No Resistance Runs," where price moves rapidly toward a target because all opposing discount or premium arrays have been invalidated.**

### Key Pillars
* **The Anatomy of a Trend**
    * A bearish trend is strictly defined by the market respecting **Premium Arrays** (sell zones) and disrespecting **Discount Arrays** (buy zones), while a bullish trend does the opposite [[01:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=80)].
    * The primary PD (Premium/Discount) arrays used to define these zones are **Fair Value Gaps (FVG)**, **Swing Points** (highs/lows), and **Fair Value Areas** (retracement zones) [[01:43](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=103)].
* **The Ping-Pong Effect**
    * Markets do not move in random retracements; instead, they "ping-pong" between resistance areas. In a bearish trend, price hits a Discount Array (resistance) to trigger a retracement back into a Premium Array before continuing lower [[04:46](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=286)].
    * Attempting to trade against this effect—such as shorting directly into a Discount Array—is described as "swimming upstream" and often leads to unnecessary losses [[07:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=444)].
* **Two-Candle Rejection (The Validation Signal)**
    * Validation of an array is determined by "Candle Science." If the first candle into a zone doesn't reject, the second candle must "sweep" the first's low/high and then reject to keep the zone valid [[09:46](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=586)].
    * When the market closes comfortably beyond these two-candle rejection points, the resistance is officially "disrespected" and removed from the chart, signaling a shift in momentum [[11:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=677)].

### The Golden Nuggets
* **Resistance as a "Bump in the Road":** In a trending market, opposing arrays aren't meant to reverse the trend, but they *will* cause a temporary reaction. Ignore them at your peril [[03:55](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=235)].
* **The Velocity Secret:** Price moves fastest when there is "nothing stopping it." A "No Resistance Run" occurs in the vacuum created after a Fair Value Area is invalidated and before the next major swing point or FVG exists [[13:14](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=794)].
* **Body vs. Wick:** When marking Fair Value Areas, use the wick if it overlaps with an FVG; if it does not overlap, use the candle body for a more accurate resistance reading [[03:01](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IA_N-icYiAs&t=181)].

### The Final Verdict
Success in trading relies on waiting for the specific moment when two-candle rejection fails and clears the path for a high-speed move to the next target. By avoiding entries into active resistance and focusing on "No Resistance Runs," traders can align themselves with the path of least resistance for faster, more reliable payouts.
