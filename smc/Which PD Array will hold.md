# The Secret to Knowing if a PD Array Will Hold

This summary outlines the methodology presented by Arjo for determining the high-probability success of **Price Delivery Arrays (PD Arrays)**. The core strategy revolves around analyzing the **intention and strength of the retracement** into these levels.

-----

## 1\. Core Principles of PD Array Strength

  * **The "Push" Analogy:** The market cannot hide its intention. If price moves aggressively (creating Fair Value Gaps) *against* your PD Array, that array is less likely to hold.
  * **Current Lag Focus:** Only analyze PD Arrays that are part of the *most recent* price action (the current swing high to swing low). Older arrays are lower probability.
  * **Timeframe Hierarchy:** Start with the higher timeframe (HTF) to find the array, then move down one timeframe for entry confirmation.

-----

## 2\. Step 1: Fair Value Areas (Blocks)

*Order Blocks, Mitigation Blocks, and Breaker Blocks.*

  * **Intention Check:** When price returns to an Order Block, watch the 3-candle pattern. If the third candle creates a bearish FVG pushing *into* your bullish block, the block is "dangerous" and likely to fail.
  * **Neutralizing the Gap:** Ideally, you want to see the third candle trade back into the first candle's low/high, effectively preventing an opposing FVG from forming.
  * **The "Disrespected" Rule:** If an opposing FVG *is* created, do not enter until that FVG is closed through (disrespected).

-----

## 3\. Step 2: Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

The strength of an FVG is dictated by the **Third Candle** of the expansion move.

  * **Small vs. Large FVGs:** If the third candle retraces deeply, leaving only a tiny sliver of the original gap, it signals heavy opposing momentum. This FVG will likely be pierced deeply or fail entirely.
  * **Confirmation Pairings:** When an FVG looks weak, use the following timeframe combinations for a new FVG in your direction before entering:
      * **Daily FVG** $\rightarrow$ Wait for **4-hour FVG**
      * **4-hour FVG** $\rightarrow$ Wait for **15-minute FVG**
      * **15-minute FVG** $\rightarrow$ Wait for **1-minute FVG**

-----

## 4\. Step 3: Swing Points (Highs and Lows)

To determine if a swing point will hold or be "swept," use **Candle Science**.

  * **Liquidity Sweeps:** A sweep is confirmed if the candle fails to close above/below the swing high/low and leaves a long wick.
  * **Fractal Intention:** On a lower timeframe, a sweep appears as price pushing past a high but then immediately creating aggressive FVGs in the *opposite* direction.
  * **Targeting:** If a swing high is swept and price starts respecting new lower-timeframe FVGs, the original swing low becomes the target.

-----

## 5\. Summary Table: Confirmation Logic

| Scenario | Indicator of Weakness | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Order Block** | Opposing FVG forms during the retracement. | Wait for the opposing FVG to be disrespected. |
| **Fair Value Gap** | Third candle creates a massive rejection/deep wick. | Wait for a new FVG on the next lower timeframe. |
| **Swing Point** | Price closes strongly past the point without a wick. | Re-evaluate; the trend is likely continuing, not reversing. |

> **Final Motto:** "Whenever in doubt, wait for a new Fair Value Gap."
