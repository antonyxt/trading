**The required confirmation for a trade entry is not static; it must scale based on the presence of opposing technical signals and the specific "probability tier" of the PD Array being traded.**

### **Key Pillars**

* **Candle Science & The "Opposing Side" Logic**
    * Traders often fail by "marrying a bias" and ignoring signs that price wants to move elsewhere [[01:27](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=87)].
    * A **Respect Candle** occurs when price wicks an unmitigated opposing PD Array (Premium Array in a bullish trend) without closing above it [[05:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=313)].
    * On a lower time frame, this singular wick represents a sequence of Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) creating a reversal, signaling that the market may respect the opposing level rather than your intended trade direction [[05:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=354)].

* **The Three Probability Tiers**
    * **High Probability:** Price approaches your PD Array without respecting any opposing arrays and without creating counter-trend FVGs [[11:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=667)].
    * **Mid Probability:** Price respects an opposing PD Array (via candle science/wicks) while retracing into your entry zone [[07:18](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=438)].
    * **Low Probability:** Price respects an opposing array *and* creates a new counter-trend FVG, providing double the evidence against your trade [[09:58](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=598)].

* **The "Sharp Turn" Baseline**
    * A Sharp Turn is the standard confirmation requirement, defined as an FVG leading *into* a PD Array followed immediately by an FVG moving *out* of it [[13:40](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=820)].
    * The baseline pairings for Sharp Turns are: Monthly PD/Daily entry, Weekly PD/4H entry, Daily PD/1H entry, 4H PD/15m entry, and 15m PD/1m entry [[12:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=742)].

### **The Entry Confirmation Process**

1.  **Identify the PD Array:** Locate the higher time frame level (e.g., a 4H FVG) you wish to trade [[02:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=133)].
2.  **Audit the Retracement:** Check if the move into your zone respected an unmitigated opposing "Premium" or "Discount" array [[03:33](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=213)].
3.  **Apply the Scaling Rule:**
    * **High Prob:** Use the standard baseline Sharp Turn (e.g., 15m entry for a 4H PD) [[19:33](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=1173)].
    * **Mid Prob:** Move up one time frame for confirmation (e.g., use a 1H Sharp Turn for a 4H PD) [[14:35](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=875)].
    * **Low Prob:** Require "Double Confirmation"—wait for a Sharp Turn on a higher time frame *plus* an additional FVG breakout from that turn [[17:16](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=1036)].

### **Golden Nuggets**

* **The Net Loss Trap:** It is possible to identify the winning direction but still end the day at a net loss by taking 2–3 "early" losses before the move finally validates [[08:46](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=526)].
* **Unmitigated Only:** An opposing PD Array is only valid for "Candle Science" if it hasn't been traded into before; mitigated levels lose their accuracy as reversal signals [[04:06](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=246)].
* **Time Frame Proximity:** The closer your entry time frame is to your PD Array time frame, the less total confirmation is needed because the data is more aligned [[14:55](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8mPlORPHo-w&t=895)].

### **The Final Verdict**
To achieve long-term profitability, traders must stop using the same entry trigger for every setup and instead demand more evidence when price shows "respect" to the opposing side. Success is found not just in catching winners, but in using scaled confirmation to avoid the premature losses that occur when price struggles against counter-trend barriers.
