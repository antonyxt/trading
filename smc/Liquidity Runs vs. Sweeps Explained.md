This video, "The Crucial Difference Between a ‘Run or Sweep of Liquidity’," is the third episode of the "Pips to Payouts" series by Arjo. It focuses on identifying whether the market is likely to reverse or continue based on how it interacts with liquidity levels.

### **Core Concepts of Liquidity**
* **What is Liquidity?** Liquidity is represented by **swing highs, swing lows, previous candle highs, and previous candle lows** [[01:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=80)]. These are areas where large numbers of orders (buy/sell stops) are clustered.
* **Institutional Story:** The market runs on the liquidity of large institutions and hedge funds, not individual retail traders [[03:31](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=211)]. Large orders require a matching order on the opposite side to be filled; for every buyer, there must be a seller [[10:32](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=632)].

### **Run vs. Sweep: The Critical Distinction**
The video explains that how price reacts after crossing a liquidity level determines the next move.

| Feature | **Run on Liquidity** | **Sweep of Liquidity** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Price Action** | Price stays and becomes "comfortable" beyond the level [[15:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=902)]. | Price moves beyond the level but is immediately rejected, leaving a long wick [[10:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=613)]. |
| **Market Intent** | Indicates **continuation** toward the next target [[15:08](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=908)]. | Indicates a **reversal** as liquidity is used to fuel a move in the opposite direction [[00:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=17)]. |
| **The "Sting"** | Often involves two "stings" or touches that establish comfort beyond the low/high [[14:28](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=868)]. | Usually an aggressive, single-sting move that "suffocates" trapped traders [[12:47](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=767)]. |


### **Practical Application & Strategy**
* **Refining Options:** Trading is about removing possibilities. If price runs a low, the bullish option is removed; if it sweeps, the bearish option is removed [[09:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=550)].
* **Trapped Traders:** In a sweep, winning institutions "suffocate" losing traders by not allowing the price to return to their entry point (the original low/high), forcing them to exit at a loss, which provides the exit liquidity for the winners [[12:53](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=773)].
* **Two-Candle Rejection:** This concept helps identify sweeps. A long wick (rejection) into a PD Array (Premium/Discount Array) suggests a sweep, while a solid body closing through it suggests a potential run [[16:32](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=992)].
* **Targeting:** Once a rejection high or low is "run," it confirms the likely direction toward the next major target or Fair Value Gap (FVG) [[17:15](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=1035)].

The video concludes by advising traders to perform case studies to recognize these patterns objectively, as this provides control and removes guesswork from trading [[20:11](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fom_7tuVqmc&t=1211)].
