**Market structure is a "magnetic" framework that identifies specific trading ranges, next likely targets, and high-probability entry points by treating intermediate highs and lows as the primary draw on liquidity.**

### Key Pillars

* **The Anatomy of Intermediate Structure**
    * **ITH/ITL Definition:** An Intermediate Term Low (ITL) is a three-candle swing low flanked by higher swing lows; an Intermediate Term High (ITH) is a swing high flanked by lower swing highs.
    * **The "Outsider" Perspective:** Effective bias is determined by viewing these as the "longest swings" on the chart, where trading above ITHs signals bullishness and trading below ITLs signals bearishness.

* **The Magnet Principle and Liquidity Pools**
    * **The Draw on Price:** ITHs and ITLs act as magnets because they contain concentrated buy/sell orders (liquidity) from institutional stop-losses and breakout traders.
    * **Probability Shifts:** Trading *toward* a magnet has a high probability (80-90% as it nears the target), whereas predicting the reaction *after* a magnet is hit has a low probability (~33%).

* **Range-Based Trading Execution**
    * **The "In-Between" Rule:** High-probability setups only exist when price is positioned between a confirmed ITH and ITL; trading outside these bounds increases the risk of being caught in consolidation.
    * **The Three Zones:** The middle of a range is a "Red Area" (wait), the approach is the "Orange Area" (prepare), and the final run toward the target is the "Green Area" (execute).

* **Precision Entry via PD Array Disrespect**
    * **The Catalyst:** Entries are triggered when price "disrespects" (blasts through) the last Fair Value Gap (FVG) or Order Block that stands between the current price and the target magnet.
    * **Filtering Retracements:** By waiting for the counter-trend support/resistance to fail, you ensure you are entering at "peak momentum" rather than catching a falling knife.

* **Multi-Timeframe Fractal Alignment**
    * **Trader Specialization:** Swing traders utilize Daily/4H structure, day traders focus on 1H/15M, and scalpers (flow traders) refine entries on the 1M chart.
    * **The Domino Effect:** Success relies on identifying the higher timeframe magnet (the "Why") and using lower timeframe structure to time the entry (the "When").

### The Golden Nuggets

* **The Magnet Acceleration:** Price action typically moves fastest just before hitting a target; if the move is sluggish near the magnet, the trade idea is likely invalid.
* **The 33% Trap:** Most traders lose money trying to predict reversals at structural points; the pro-tip is to capture the "easy" meat in the middle of the range.
* **Aggressive Risk Management:** Move stop-losses to break-even the moment the structural magnet is reached, as these areas are prone to immediate, volatile consolidations.

### The Final Verdict

The ultimate objective of a trader is to identify the strongest magnetic draw on liquidity and capture the high-momentum "snap" toward that target. By ignoring the middle of ranges and only entering when the final obstacle (PD Array) is disrespected, you align yourself with institutional flow for high-RR payouts.
