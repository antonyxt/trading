**The most effective use of Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) is not finding support to "bounce" from, but identifying the "walls" of opposing momentum that must be removed before a high-probability trade can ignite.**

### Key Pillars
* **The Paradigm Shift: From "Support" to "Obstacles"**
    * Traditional FVG trading focuses on what price can "continue higher from," whereas professional trading focuses on what is currently "preventing" price from moving [[01:29](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=89)].
    * FVGs often fail or lead to messy consolidations because traders ignore the "wall" (an opposing FVG or swing high/low) standing in the way of the desired direction [[02:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=137)].
    * A bullish FVG only becomes high probability once the bearish "wall" preventing upward movement is definitively overtaken [[02:45](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=165)].

* **The Fallacy of the Retracement**
    * Waiting for a retracement back into an FVG is counterintuitive because an FVG represents aggressive momentum; a retracement actually introduces opposing momentum [[05:45](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=345)].
    * Traders waiting for retracements are often "behind the curve," trying to call bottoms while the easiest, most explosive trades happen when price is moving aggressively alongside momentum [[06:21](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=381)].
    * The "Ping Pong Effect" occurs when price bounces between a support FVG and a resistance swing high, creating 50/50 probability traps [[08:48](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=528)].

* **The "Two-Candle Rejection" Mechanic**
    * To confirm a "wall" is no longer preventing movement, traders must monitor the first or second candle that trades into a key level (swing high/low or opposing FVG) [[12:47](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=767)].
    * A valid rejection should see immediate follow-through; if price trades back above the high of that rejection candle, the obstacle is considered disrespected or "filled" [[14:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=862)].
    * Once this failure occurs, price is "free to run" without needing a further retracement back into a support FVG [[16:31](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=991)].

### Golden Nuggets
* **The "One-Possibility" Rule:** High-probability trading is like a crime scene; you must remove all "opposing possibilities" (bearish scenarios) until only one direction remains logically sound [[08:26](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=506)].
* **The Wall Analogy:** If you are running toward a wall, no matter how much force is pushing you from behind (the FVG), you won't move forward until the wall is removed [[02:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=125)].
* **Momentum Alignment:** The most satisfying trades are those that "explode" immediately after entry because they occur after the final "wall" has been breached [[07:12](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9lbglU1jdQ&t=432)].

### The Final Verdict
Stop viewing Fair Value Gaps as static safety nets and start using them as indicators of market pressure that must clear opposing "walls" to unlock explosive moves. True profitability lies in trading alongside active momentum rather than trying to catch falling knives during retracements.
